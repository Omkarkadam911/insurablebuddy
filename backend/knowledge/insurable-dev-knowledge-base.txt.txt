ABOUT INSURABLE.DEV
==================================================

Insurable.dev provides AI agents for life insurance distribution.

The core mission of Insurable.dev is to decouple premium growth from headcount by scaling insurance distribution with autonomous AI agents.

The platform focuses on:
- Automating lead qualification
- Accelerating underwriting
- Empowering human agents to close more deals

Insurable.dev enables 24/7 availability, built-in compliance, and scalable insurance operations using autonomous AI systems.

==================================================
CORE VALUE PROPOSITION
==================================================

Insurable.dev helps insurance organizations scale without hiring more agents.

Autonomous AI agents handle lead qualification, outbound engagement, speed-to-lead, and underwriting support so human agents spend more time closing high-intent prospects instead of performing repetitive outreach and filtering tasks.

The result is higher appointment volume, faster conversions, and lower customer acquisition cost.

THE DISTRIBUTION CRISIS
==================================================

Insurable.dev describes a distribution crisis in insurance driven by three core problems:
1. Speed-to-lead gaps
2. Rising customer acquisition costs
3. Failure of static digital forms

--------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM 1: SPEED-TO-LEAD GAP

Insurable.dev claims:
- Contact rates drop by 400% within the first hour after a lead is generated
- Typical time to first contact is 24–48 hours
- Prospects often contact 3–5 competitors before an agent responds

Manual dialing delays reduce conversions and cause lost opportunities.

--------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM 2: THE CAC TRAP

Key claims include:
- Agents spend approximately 70% of their time on unqualified leads
- 50–100 calls are required to book one appointment
- Cost per qualified appointment ranges from $200–$400
- Annual agent burnout ranges from 30–40%

Premium growth becomes limited by hiring, training, and agent availability.

--------------------------------------------------

PROBLEM 3: FAILURE OF STATIC FORMS

Insurable.dev states that traditional D2C forms:
- Have 70–85% abandonment rates
- Achieve only 15–30% completion rates
- Are confusing due to insurance jargon
- Are long, rigid, and lack real-time guidance
